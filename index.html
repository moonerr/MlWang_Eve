# Team's perspective

Stress can cause harm to people’s mental and physical health and reduce the overall happiness of society (1).

Sometimes it requires too much for individuals to grow to meet the requirements for the development of society. Many works have done by digital technologies to improve efficiency, cooperativeness and fantasy. We definitely could make some changes to the environment just for people to relieve themselves.

As a semester project, we are supposed to build one prototype to demonstrate our concepts. Even though we found being stressful can occur to anyone, we still need to focus on a specific group to tailor the requirements and constraints to practical design work. We choose young adults, not only for they are facing much stress and also for it is more accessible for us to observe their pressure and talk with them about their emotions. 🌝 Intended Concept

My solution for future Mundane to this problem is to build a private space for the public. It should be accessible for everybody as destressing would become a natural needs for everyone in a crazy assumption of the future.
When someone is entering, this space begins to work. Users can set the brightness of the whole to suit them best. The entire area has a cylindrical shape with a soft floor and round wall.
They could stand, sit or lie down; with either posture, they could find their way to throw, squeeze, slap the ball-shaped bodies(planets) to generate multiple changes to this space. These changes include vibration, colours, surrounding sound.
It is a room without intructions and what needs to be done. Just a set of various interactions for people to explore.
And with the interactive process, everyone could get their stress released by multi-sensory therapy.

How does the suround sound work?

Hardware


  1. Force Sensitive Resistors (FSR) * 2

    2. FSR is Used for detecting the pressure applied by two hands. It converts the force to an analog voltage. Arduino converts the analog voltage into digital one, ranged from 0 to 1023. The harder users squeeze FSR, the bigger the number is.

  2. Arduino Uno

    3. It is a basic set for makers, powered by a USB porter or any 5V power port. It is used as a controler to cope with inputs and outputs. It receives the signals from FSR and sends them to laptop(Processing).

  3. Arduino Lilypad

    4. Lilypad gives back its acceleratio in Y-axis when it rolls. And it will send to processing as a input to control the balance of music.

  4. Laptop (processing) with speakers

    5. Lap top is used for executing Processing code and give out sound with its two-channel speakers.

# Code with Arduino

  1. Read the value from FSR

    2. Connect the FSR to Arduino with A0, A1 as input port. Get the value from the two pins by method analogRead(PIN). 

                            
                        int fsrAnalogPinLeft = A0; // Left FSR is connected to analog 0
                        int fsrAnalogPinRight = A1; // Right FSR is connected to anolog 1
                        int fsrReadingLeft, fsrReadingRight;      // the analog reading from the FSR resistor divider
            
                        void setup() {
                        // Init USB serial port for debugging
                        Serial.begin(9600);
                        }
            
                        void loop() {
                        //get the fst number
                        fsrReadingLeft = analogRead(fsrAnalogPinLeft);
                        fsrReadingRight = analogRead(fsrAnalogPinRight);
                        }
  2. Prepare the value for communication with Processing

    3. The virable Adjust is created to calculate the difference between the values of pressure for each side. Thus, the first step is to use one value to minus the other.
    Then because the default frame rate of Processing is 60, which is to say, it will read about every 20ms. So secondly, it is essential to set a timmer for the arduino to send the value every 50ms, which should be no less than 20ms.
    It is also important to check the writing capability of Arduino Uno. While using Serial.write(), the data is sent by One byte each time. So it is why in here Adjust was mapped to 0 ~ 28-1. 

                            
                        //set a timer
                        unsigned long timer = 0;
                        void loop() {
                            ...
                            
                            //prepare the value of adjuat
                            int Adjust;
                            Adjust = (fsrReadingRight - fsrReadingLeft);//The stronger side wins
                            Adjust = map(Adjust, -1000, 1000,0,255); //map to fit the O/I limitation
                            
                            //Let the arduino send the value every 50ms
                            if((millis()-timer)>50){ 
                                if (fsrReadingRight != 0 || fsrReadingLeft != 0){
                                    Serial.write(Adjust);}
                                timer = millis();  
                            }
                        }

  3. Play the music with balance

    The last step, use playerN.pan(AdjustN) to control the music balance!

How did I made the ball?

Enhance the mini sensor

# Fix the Force Sensitive Resistor

The FSR is only one 2 AUD coin-sized. To expand the contact surface, I get a good idea from Lorna.
The foam of the playmat is an attractive choice to wrap the sensor, for it is solid but relatively resilient. It will fix and protect the sensor and give a bigger contact surface for each ball.

The video shows how soft foam works in resilience but lacks solid support. It would be easy to damage the sensor if users squeeze the sensor at an inappropriate angle.

# Fix the Micro Vibrating Motor

And the vibrating motor (VM) is even more fragile for its far thinner wires. But thanks to the self-adjusting wire stripper, I did not cut the wire anymore. Tools are great!

I first dig a VM-shaped hole in the cut foam piece to fix the VM because VM is a bit thick if we put two pieces together directly. And then adhere them all to make it a core for the ball.
The foam pieces are tailored to a fist big to suit users' hand size. The secondary layer will be smaller to make the whole a ball shape.

Encapsulate the Ball

# Wrap with sponge

After making the core ready, it's time to wrap it up. The sponge we decided on has been tested that it can allow the lights to get through. Then to wrap the core, I tailored two oval pieces of sponge and sewed them as a pocket. My hands are always full so that there are not many pictures left, but I could show you how I sewed it from an oval flat pocket into a ball shape.

I got inspiration from the method of making a globe.

  1. I first cut out a plane map of the earth. The length of this plane is the circumference of the target sphere, and the width is half of the circumference. Then divide it into four equal parts.
  2. First, stitch out the symmetrical sides.
  3. Then there is the back.
  4. Then turn it inside out. Put in the core part and fill cotton at both ends in the direction of pressing. Finally, stitch the last edge.

# Wrap with cloth




One time I tried to wrap them with balloons, but it affected the value of FSR, and then I adjust the mapping of it. But it still did not work well on the spot. If only I could get a larger one, I think it would be working in some way.



Coming to the use of cloth, it is very similar to using a sponge. But because it is the outermost layer, very delicate handwork is required.


First, cut out two circles from paper. The diameter of the circle is 3/2 that of the target ball. And then cut out the overlayed part of the circle and make it a bare board.
Then outlines the shapes of the board on the cloth and cut them out of 4 pieces.
The fabric is very elastic, verified by my teammates that sewing machines would not help.
So I, a craftsman, sew two and a half cloth covers. To make it look best, I sewed the four pieces of cloth into a spherical shape by the hidden needle stitching method.
After finishing wrapping, they are sent to be coloured. I coloured one ball with acrylic paint. It needs to be diluted to a particular concentration not to affect the light transmittance.

Design for the stressed

  1. What is stress?

    2. Stress that cannot be digested is considered harmful and happens anytime and anywhere.

    With three assignments due in a week, we feel it.

    Being losing money for one month, we sense it.

    When the project is due tomorrow, but countless bugs are found, we are caught by it.

    Previous researchers have helped us understand that stress arises from individuals who estimate insufficient resources to solve the current dilemma. This is closely related to the pressure exerted by the environment on people and depends on the individual's emotional management ability.

  2. What works to cope with it?

    3. Positive emotions can help adapt stress level by sustaining coping efforts, providing a “breather,” and restoring depleted resources.(Folkman & Moskowitz, 2000; LAZARUS et al., 1980)

    Positive emotions can be generated in three ways: Positive Reappraisal, Problem-Focused Coping methods, and Creation of Positive Events. (Folkman & Moskowitz, 2000)

    'Multisensory therapy' is a prooved treatment, making the users stay in a private room and exposed to interactions with feedback in various senses. It also has another name, 'snoezelen' in Dutch, which combines the 'explore' and 'relax'.

    It would be a fantastic realm to explore as it gives me opportunities to break the everyday mundane at present. From an elementary observation, people use eyes too often and with noise from the automobile all around the street. There is much that can be done to reverse the stressful reality in one cozy space.

  3. Related Work

    4. Fidget toys are popular among young adults, and it gives them a way to ventilate the stress. Not only soft toys are welcomes. People in China are fond of playing antique walnuts in hand and roll them infinitely. The simple way of playing could distract people's attention from stressful stuff thus get relaxed(18 Fidget Toys for Anxiety, 2021). Distraction that shuts down one emotion (Verduyn et al., 2015).

    The massage gun provides a way to relax the muscles. When people are nervous or after sitting for a long time, their muscles tend to be tight. The constant touchable vibration relaxes the muscles and improves mood because real life tells us that very few people lose their temper when being massaged.

    Galaxy Exhibition: Musical pieces are played throughout the venue that change in sequence depending on where the audience member is standing.(Galaxy of Records, 2017)

    Multi-sensory Exposure Therapy, which uses 3D Visualisation, Surround Sound, and Synthesised Music can help anxiety healing. (Argo et al., 2014)

Design Guidelines

  1. Behavioural Design

    2. According to Dornald A. Norman, behavioural design reveals the part of brain which controls human's behaviours.

    Venting Behaviour

    • Freud (1955) proposed the principle of constancy, When we feel stressed, the quantity of excitation increases, but our body wants us to decrease it, which leads us to seeking a way to vent emotions.
    • Shock, anger, fear, nervousness... these emotions are all high arousal emotions, ... It increases blood pressure and heart rate, alerts our sensory system, and also helps us become ready to make response
    • Our brain can’t afford long term stress because keeping long, term high arousal increases the burden of blood vessels in the brain.
    • Venting can make people feel better and calm after doing it

    Fidget Behaviour

    • Fidgeting is defined as the restless act of moving that is not necessary to ongoing events or tasks
    • fidgeting distracts the part of our brain that has become unpleasant so other parts can pay attention to what we are doing.


  2. Emotional Design

    3. Emotional level reveals how people feel about it. To cope with stress, we need to know what causes happiness.

    The Assumtions about Shapes and Froms

    • triangle is stable.
    • square is solemn
    • circle is perfect
    • geometric shape is simple and cold
    • curves remind us freedom and nature
    • The combination of different shapes gives people more emotional experiences

    Smooth Curve

        straight lines with angles are widely used to describe angry, exciting and agitation.
    • On the contrary, curvy lines express grace and are usually pleasant, in Lundholm (1921)’s experiment
    • adding smooth curvy line elements

    Colours

    • higher color saturation also makes people feel more excited and human arousal increased when hue changed: the order is from blue and green to red
    • have more than three colors, too many colors will give people a “busy” feeling

    Cuteness

    • animals, plants and infants as inspirations
    • the concept “baby schema”- a set of infantile physical features such as large head, high and protruding forehead, large eyes, chubby cheeks, small nose and mouth, short and thick extremities and plump body shape, such as “Pikachu” and “Mickey Mouse”
    • “cute aggression” which refers to an aggressive behavior caused by seeing something cute --- People experiencing this may grit their teeth, clench their fists or feel the desire of squeezing the thing what they consider is cute without the intention of actually causing any harm

Key insights & Reflection

  • 'Galaxy' is an imaginative theme that often makes people think of hugeness and distance. We received much feedback that we should increase the number of planets. So in the final exhibition, we made seven balls hanging in the space. But it turns out that many things should be placed in an ample space as well. Working people, often young, usually spend 8 to 10 hours a day at the workplace, which occupies 1/3 of the whole day, even more than their sleeping time. If sleeping should be paid attention to, it seems reasonable to consider improving the experience during workdays.
